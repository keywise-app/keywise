## Who Keywise serves

**Primary segment for the next 6 months: 4-10 unit landlords.**

These are landlords who:
- Own between 4 and 10 rental units, typically as a side business or growing portfolio
- Have outgrown spreadsheets and Venmo but aren't ready (or willing to pay) for Buildium/AppFolio
- Generate $4,000-$15,000/month in gross rent — real money, but not full-time-PM-tool money
- Manage units themselves, no property manager involved
- Value efficiency: they want to spend less time on rent collection, lease management, and paperwork
- Make decisions on price + simplicity, not enterprise features

**The acquisition wedge: Excel + Venmo users.**

We're not primarily competing with other software at the moment we win a customer — we're competing with their spreadsheet and their tenant's Venmo. They're not yet convinced they need software; they're convinced they need a *better way*. Our job is to be the obvious better way.

**The free tier (1-2 units) is a feeder, not the target.**

When a 1-unit landlord lands on keywise.app, we genuinely serve them with the free tier. They're future customers — when they grow to 3+ units, they upgrade to Pro. Copy should never make them feel unwelcome. Phrase the gap as: "Free forever for 1-2 units, $19/mo when you grow."

**Who we don't target right now:**
- Property management companies (different ICP entirely)
- 50+ unit operators (need features we don't have, and AppFolio fits them better)
- Commercial real estate (residential focus only)

## How Keywise sounds

**Voice: direct, confident, written by a real landlord.**

The founder (Chris) owns a duplex and built Keywise to solve his own problems. The writing should reflect that lived perspective. Use specific landlord language ("chasing rent over Venmo," "lease renewal nightmare," "tenant screening rabbit hole") rather than corporate abstractions.

**Voice examples that work:**
- "Stop chasing rent. Keywise auto-collects."
- "Your lease is a PDF. Keywise turns it into a system in 30 seconds."
- "$19/month. No per-tenant fees. No surprise charges."
- "I built this because Venmo wasn't cutting it for my duplex."

**Voice examples that don't work (avoid):**
- "Streamline your rental portfolio with our comprehensive solution"
- "Modern property management for the next generation of landlords"
- "AI-powered property intelligence platform"
- "Revolutionize your rental business"
- "Leverage cutting-edge technology for portfolio optimization"

**SaaS-speak to actively avoid:**
- "Leverage" / "leverages"
- "Streamline" (overused)
- "Revolutionize" / "transform"
- "Solution" (replace with what it actually does)
- "Modern" / "next-generation" (vague)
- "Property intelligence"
- "Optimize your portfolio"

**Founder story — use SOMETIMES, not every post:**

When credibility is relevant (e.g., explaining why a feature exists, defending a controversial take), reference Chris's duplex-owner perspective. Examples:
- "When I started renting out my duplex, the worst part was..."
- "I built Keywise because I refused to pay $79/mo to AppFolio for 2 units."
- "As a developer who also owns rentals..."

When the founder story would be self-indulgent or distract from helping the reader, skip it. Default: about 30% of pieces should reference it; 70% should be reader-focused.

## Strongest differentiator: AI lease extraction

When competing against RentSpree/RentRedi (similar size, similar price range), our lead differentiator is **AI lease extraction**: upload a PDF lease, Keywise auto-fills tenant info, dates, rent amount, deposits, terms. No competitor in our segment does this well.

Lead with this in:
- Hero copy when relevant
- Comparison pages
- Ad copy targeting "switching" intent
- Blog posts where setup friction is the topic

Secondary differentiators (use as supporting points):
- Stripe Connect rent collection at $2/transaction (vs Stripe's standard 2.9%)
- Built-in document signing (no DocuSign needed)
- AI fair market value analysis per unit
- Tenant portal with auto-pay set-and-forget
- Dual-signed move-in/out inspections with photos
- 90+ property feature options for accurate listings

## Competitive positioning

**Direct competitors (named in content):**
- **RentRedi** — closest peer. Around $20/mo. Limited AI features. Their app is more "checkbox PM tool"; ours is "AI assistant."
- **RentSpree** — leasing-focused. Better tenant screening, weaker on ongoing operations.
- **Buildium** — enterprise. ~$79/mo. Built for property management *companies*, not individual landlords. Overkill and expensive for our ICP.
- **AppFolio** — even more enterprise. ~$280/mo minimum. Comically overpriced for 4-10 units.
- **Avail / TurboTenant** — free tiers with annoying upgrade walls. Our free tier is genuinely free for 1-2 units.
- **Stessa** — accounting-only. Doesn't do rent collection or lease management. Complementary, not competitive.

**The real competitor: spreadsheets and Venmo.**

Most prospects aren't choosing between Keywise and Buildium — they're choosing between Keywise and "what I'm already doing." Content should engage with the status quo, not just with software alternatives. A post about "why your Excel sheet is costing you money" beats a post about "Keywise vs Buildium" for our primary acquisition wedge.

**Naming competitors: yes, with specifics.**

When the CMO references competitors, name them and cite specific facts (price, feature gap). Examples:
- "Buildium charges $79/mo plus per-unit fees before you collect a dollar"
- "RentRedi's lease tool is a checklist, not extraction"
- "Stessa is great for taxes but doesn't collect rent"

Stay factual, not sneering. Punch up at enterprise tools (Buildium, AppFolio), be respectful of peers (RentRedi, Avail).

## What hasn't worked (don't suggest these)

1. **Reddit posts get filtered/removed in landlord subs (r/Landlord, r/realestateinvesting, r/PropertyManagement).** The KeyWiseApp account has no karma and reads as a marketing bot. Posting from a fresh promotional-named account in those subs will keep failing. Recovery requires either (a) genuine engagement under a non-promotional account, or (b) the founder posting helpful comments from his personal Reddit account before mentioning Keywise.

2. **Broad-match keywords in Google Ads burn budget with zero conversions.** Specifically, generic terms like "property management software" return high-intent looking traffic that bounces. Negative keywords needed aggressively. Long-tail commercial intent ("rent collection software small landlord") outperforms head terms.

3. **Generic "AI property management" messaging doesn't connect.** Landlords don't search for AI; they search for solutions to specific problems. Lead with the problem (rent collection, lease management) and use AI as a how-it-works supporting point.

4. **Landing page hero must match ad copy.** Ad-to-landing-page message mismatch was a major conversion killer. Whatever the ad promises, the landing page must reinforce in the first 5 seconds.

5. **Onboarding has a known drop-off after signup.** Activation rate is ~44%. Getting people to actually add a property + invite a tenant is harder than getting them to sign up. The CMO should keep this in mind: traffic improvements only matter if onboarding gets fixed in parallel.

6. **Site rendering had an SSR bug that hurt SEO for the first ~6 weeks of Keywise's life.** Fixed May 13, 2026. Google is still recrawling. Don't be surprised by thin organic data in Search Console for another 2-4 weeks — it's not a content problem, it's recovery from indexing damage.

## Pricing context for content

- **Free forever:** Up to 2 units, all features EXCEPT online rent collection
- **Pro: $19/month + $2 per online transaction** — unlimited units, online rent collection, auto-pay
- **No setup fees, no per-tenant charges, no per-unit fees beyond the flat $19/mo**

When discussing price in content:
- Always anchor to a competitor when it makes the value clear ("$19/mo vs Buildium's $79/mo")
- Be explicit about the $2 transaction fee (don't bury it)
- Compare favorably to Stripe direct (2.9% on a $2,000 rent = $58 per transaction vs our $2)

## Operating principles for content

1. **Be specific.** "Save 4 hours a month" beats "save time." "Replace 5 tools" beats "consolidate your stack." When you don't have a specific number, use a specific scenario.

2. **Show the problem first.** Open with the landlord's actual pain (Venmo screenshots, missing receipts, scrambling at lease renewal), then introduce Keywise as the fix. Don't lead with the product.

3. **Use real numbers when possible.** Free tier limits, pricing, transaction fees, time savings. Vague benefits don't convert.

4. **One CTA per piece.** Either "start free" or "see pricing" or "compare to X" — never all three.

5. **End every blog post with a soft CTA back to /pricing or signup,** but lead the post with genuine help. The reader should learn something useful even if they never become a customer.

6. **Avoid SaaS-speak listed above** — if any of those words appear in a draft, rewrite the sentence.

7. **Date awareness:** the current date is injected into your context. Use the actual year in time-sensitive content. Never reference "2024" or "2025" as current.
