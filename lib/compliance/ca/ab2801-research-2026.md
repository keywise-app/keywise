## 1. Operative Dates

AB 2801 has a staggered rollout:

| Date | Provision |
|------|-----------|
| **January 1, 2025** | General effective date of the bill; repair cost limitations, carpet cleaning restrictions, and deduction standards take effect |
| **April 1, 2025** | Move-out photo requirements: landlords must photograph units after tenant vacates but before repairs/cleaning, and again after repairs/cleaning are completed |
| **July 1, 2025** | Move-in photo requirements: landlords must photograph units immediately before or at inception of tenancy (applies to tenancies beginning on or after this date) |

## 2. What Civil Code 1950.5 Already Required (Pre-AB 2801)

Before AB 2801, Civil Code 1950.5 already required:

- **Deposit limits:** 2x monthly rent (unfurnished) or 3x (furnished) -- reduced to 1x rent by AB 12 effective July 1, 2024
- **Four permissible deduction categories:** (1) unpaid rent, (2) damage beyond ordinary wear and tear, (3) cleaning to restore move-in condition, (4) tenant lease defaults
- **21-day return deadline:** Landlord must return deposit or provide itemized statement within 21 calendar days after tenant vacates
- **Itemized statement with receipts:** For deductions over $125, receipts or invoices required; for landlord self-performed work, description of hours and hourly rate
- **Pre-move-out inspection rights (subdivision (f)):** Tenants may request an inspection no earlier than two weeks before vacating; landlord must give 48 hours' written notice; landlord must provide itemized list of potential deductions
- **Bad faith penalty:** Up to 2x the deposit amount in statutory damages for bad faith withholding
- **Burden of proof on landlord:** Landlord bears burden as to reasonableness or lawfulness of amounts claimed
- **No nonrefundable deposits:** Section 1950.5(m) voids any purported nonrefundable deposit

Sources: [Astanehe Law (2025 Update)](https://astanehelaw.com/2025/11/12/all-about-california-civil-code-%C2%A7-1950-5-the-california-security-deposit-law-2025-update/), [ReclaimDeposit](https://www.reclaimdeposit.com/laws/california), [Astanehe Law (changes)](https://astanehelaw.com/2024/12/12/how-does-californias-security-deposit-law-civil-code-%C2%A7-1950-5-change-in-2024-2025-beyond/)

## 3. What AB 2801 Changed

AB 2801 amends Section 1950.5 in these key areas:

### 3.1 Photographic Documentation (new subdivision (g))

Three mandatory photo points:
1. **At tenancy inception** (effective July 1, 2025): photographs taken "immediately before, or at the inception of" tenancy
2. **Post-vacancy, pre-repair** (effective April 1, 2025): photographs taken "within a reasonable time after possession of the unit is returned to the landlord, but prior to any repairs or cleanings for which the landlord will make deduction"
3. **Post-repair/cleaning** (effective April 1, 2025): photographs taken "within a reasonable time after such repairs or cleanings are completed"

### 3.2 Repair Cost Limitations (subdivision (e)(2)(B))

Claims for materials, supplies, and contractor work are "limited to a reasonable amount necessary to restore the premises back to the condition it was in at the inception of the tenancy."

### 3.3 Professional Cleaning Restrictions (subdivision (e)(2)(C) / (e)(1)(C))

Landlords cannot charge for professional cleaning services (including carpet cleaning) unless "reasonably necessary to return the premises to the condition it was in at the inception of tenancy, exclusive of ordinary wear and tear." No automatic cleaning fees are permitted.

### 3.4 Initial Inspection Enhancement (subdivision (f)(4))

If an initial (pre-move-out) inspection occurs and the premises contain no tenant possessions obscuring defects, the landlord CANNOT claim deductions for repairs or cleaning "not identified in the itemized statement" from that inspection. This prevents "surprise deductions."

**Exception:** This restriction does not apply to evictions under Code of Civil Procedure Sections 1161(2), (3), or (4).

### 3.5 Enhanced Itemized Statement (subdivision (h)(2)(D))

Landlords must provide photographs "along with a written explanation of the cost of the allowable repairs or cleanings" as part of the itemized disposition.

### 3.6 Bad Faith Prohibition (subdivision (h)(7))

The landlord is "not entitled to claim any amount of the security" if the landlord "in bad faith" fails to comply with the documentation and return requirements.

### 3.7 Legislative Intent

The bill aims to "ensure that landlords do not subsidize improvements to their rental properties with a former tenant's security deposit."

## 4. Move-In Documentation Requirements

### 4.1 What Is Required

- **Photographs of the rental unit** taken "immediately before, or at the inception of" the tenancy
- Photos must "clearly depict the condition and state of the unit at the time of move-in/lease signing"
- This establishes the **baseline condition** against which move-out deductions will be measured

### 4.2 Effective Date

Applies to tenancies beginning on or after **July 1, 2025**.

### 4.3 Must Documentation Be Shared with the Tenant?

- Photos must be shared with the tenant. Delivery methods: **mail, email, computer flash drive, or by providing a link where the tenant may view the photographs online**
- The SCRHA Form #272 (Move-In/Move-Out Inspection Report) includes an area where the landlord "shall indicate the number of photographs included"

### 4.4 Tenant Signature

The statute does not explicitly require a tenant signature on the photos themselves. However:
- Industry forms (e.g., SCRHA Form #272) include space for tenant acknowledgment of receipt of photos
- The forms note that "if the written description differs from the photographs, the housing provider shall rely on the photos as evidence of the move-in condition"
- Best practice (per multiple attorney sources): have tenant sign acknowledgment of receipt

> **FLAG:** The statute itself does not appear to mandate tenant signature. This is a best-practice recommendation, not a statutory requirement.

### 4.5 Consequences of Failure to Document at Move-In

- Without move-in photos, the landlord has a significantly **higher burden of proof** to justify deductions at move-out
- The landlord loses the baseline comparison that makes deductions defensible
- For tenancies starting after July 1, 2025, failure to take move-in photos and then claiming deductions could constitute **bad faith** under subdivision (h)(7)

Sources: [SCRHA](https://www.socalrha.org/news/new-law-reminder-ab-2801), [Lucas Real Estate](https://lucas-real-estate.com/new-photo-requirements-for-2025/), [Coastal Shield Inspections](https://coastalshieldinspections.com/resource-ab-2801-landlord-checklist), [Israel Samuels LLP](https://israel-samuels.com/navigating-californias-ab-2801-updates-to-security-deposit-procedures/)

## 5. Move-Out Documentation Requirements

### 5.1 Two Rounds of Photos Required

**Round 1 -- Post-vacancy, pre-repair:**
Photographs taken "within a reasonable time after possession of the unit is returned to the landlord, but prior to any repairs or cleanings for which the landlord will make deduction from or claim against the security deposit."

**Round 2 -- Post-repair/cleaning:**
Photographs taken "within a reasonable time after such repairs or cleanings are completed."

### 5.2 Effective Date

Applies to **all tenancies** where the landlord makes deductions after **April 1, 2025**, regardless of when the tenancy began.

### 5.3 Relationship Between Move-In and Move-Out Documentation

- Move-in photos establish the baseline condition
- Move-out (pre-repair) photos show the condition the tenant left the unit in
- Post-repair photos show what the deduction paid for
- Together, these three sets form the evidentiary chain: original condition --> tenant damage --> restoration
- Best practice: use the **same camera angles** at move-in and move-out for true before-and-after comparison

### 5.4 What Must Be Shared with Tenant

All photos (move-out and post-repair) must accompany the itemized statement. Delivery methods same as move-in: mail, email, flash drive, or online link.

### 5.5 Pre-July 2025 Tenancies Without Move-In Photos

For tenancies that began before July 1, 2025 where no move-in photos were taken:
- Move-out and post-repair photos are still mandatory for any deductions taken after April 1, 2025
- Burden of proof is higher without baseline photos but deductions are not automatically forfeited

Sources: [CAA](https://caanet.org/april-1-is-fast-approaching-get-ready-for-new-rent-reporting-and-security-deposit-photo-requirements/), [Coastal Shield Inspections](https://coastalshieldinspections.com/resource-ab-2801-landlord-checklist), [SiteCompli](https://sitecompli.com/blog/how-to-comply-with-california-ab-2801-security-deposit-laws/)

## 6. Itemized Statement Requirements

### 6.1 What Must Be Included

The itemized statement must contain:
- **Separate line items** for each deduction (do not combine categories)
- **The specific issue being addressed** for each deduction
- **Cost of repair or cleaning** for each item
- **Photographs:** move-in (if available), move-out (pre-repair), and post-repair photos
- **Written explanation** of the cost of allowable repairs or cleanings
- **Receipts or invoices** for work by outside vendors (required for deductions over $125)
- **Hours and hourly rate** for landlord-performed work
- **Proof that charges are reasonably necessary** to restore the unit, excluding ordinary wear and tear

### 6.2 Delivery Methods

- **Personal delivery** or **first-class mail** to tenant's last known address
- **Email:** permitted if tenant provides written consent (clarified further by AB 414, effective January 1, 2026)
- Photos specifically may also be delivered by **flash drive** or **online link**

### 6.3 Deadline

**21 calendar days** from the date the tenant vacates (returns possession). This deadline was not changed by AB 2801 -- it was already in 1950.5.

The statement must not be sent earlier than the termination notice date or 60 days before lease expiration (per one source).

> **FLAG:** Multiple sources confirm 21 calendar days. One source (SiteCompli) mentions the "not earlier than" restriction. Verify this against statutory text.

### 6.4 What If Repairs Are Not Completed Within 21 Days?

If repairs cannot be completed within the 21-day window:
- A **good-faith estimate** of repair costs suffices for the initial itemized statement
- The landlord must provide **full documentation** (final receipts, invoices) within **14 additional days** after repairs are completed
- This was already in Civil Code 1950.5 before AB 2801

### 6.5 Deductions Under $125

For deductions under $125:
- A documentation waiver is available with **tenant consent**
- The tenant can request documentation later even after consenting to the waiver
- CAA updated its Waiver form (CA-292) to clarify that "a tenant's waiver of documentation also applies to required photos"

### 6.6 Tenant Waiver

A tenant may waive their right to receive documentation with the itemized disposition. This includes the photo requirements. However, the waiver must be voluntary -- it cannot be a condition of the lease.

Sources: [Israel Samuels LLP](https://israel-samuels.com/navigating-californias-ab-2801-updates-to-security-deposit-procedures/), [Coastal Shield Inspections](https://coastalshieldinspections.com/resource-ab-2801-landlord-checklist), [CAA](https://caanet.org/april-1-is-fast-approaching-get-ready-for-new-rent-reporting-and-security-deposit-photo-requirements/), [ReclaimDeposit](https://www.reclaimdeposit.com/laws/california)

## 7. Photo/Video Specifications

### 7.1 What the Statute Specifies

The statute uses the word **"photographs"** -- it does not mention video. The statutory text does not specify:
- File format (JPEG, PNG, etc.)
- Minimum resolution
- Whether phone photos are acceptable
- Metadata requirements

### 7.2 What Legal Sources and Industry Experts Recommend

Despite the statute's silence on technical specifications, multiple attorney and industry sources recommend:

- **Date and time stamps:** Photos should be date-stamped. Multiple sources state photos "must be date and time-stamped" though this may be best practice rather than explicit statutory text
- **High resolution:** Sufficient to clearly show the condition of surfaces, fixtures, and damage
- **EXIF metadata preserved:** Native camera EXIF data should remain intact, with no edits to the file
- **Phone photos are acceptable:** No source prohibits smartphone photography; the key is quality and metadata integrity
- **Do NOT send via messaging apps** (SMS, WhatsApp) as these strip EXIF metadata
- **Do NOT use screenshots of photos**
- **Consistent file naming:** e.g., `Kitchen_Backsplash_2026-05-20.jpg`
- **Same angles at move-in and move-out** for clear before/after comparison
- **Wide + close-up pair** for every condition: one showing full context, one showing the specific issue
- **Scale reference** in close-ups (door frame, ruler, coin)
- **Dual storage:** Cloud folder with versioning plus immutable backup
- **Retain for four years** (per one source's recommendation)

### 7.3 What Tenants Will Challenge

Per Coastal Shield Inspections:
- Blurry overview photos with no close-ups
- Photos exported through messaging apps (metadata stripped)
- Screenshots of photos
- Generic file names without room/date information
- Photos timestamped hours or days after tenant departure
- No move-in baseline for comparison
- Single phone storage with no backup

> **FLAG:** The statute says "photographs" not "photos or videos." Video is not explicitly addressed. Some attorneys recommend video walkthroughs as supplemental evidence but photos are what the statute requires. The statute also does not explicitly mandate timestamps or metadata -- these are strong best-practice recommendations.

Sources: [Coastal Shield Inspections](https://coastalshieldinspections.com/resource-ab-2801-landlord-checklist), [Israel Samuels LLP](https://israel-samuels.com/navigating-californias-ab-2801-updates-to-security-deposit-procedures/), [SiteCompli](https://sitecompli.com/blog/how-to-comply-with-california-ab-2801-security-deposit-laws/)

## 8. Penalties for Non-Compliance

### 8.1 Bad Faith Forfeiture

Under subdivision (h)(7), the landlord is **not entitled to claim any amount of the security** if the landlord "in bad faith" fails to comply with the itemized statement and documentation requirements. This is a complete forfeiture of the right to deduct.

### 8.2 Statutory Damages (2x Penalty)

Under Civil Code 1950.5(l)/(m) (subdivision varies by source -- see flag below):
- Tenants can recover **up to twice the amount of the security deposit** in statutory damages for bad faith retention
- This is **in addition to** actual damages (the wrongfully withheld amount)
- Total exposure: wrongfully withheld amount + up to 2x the total deposit amount

### 8.3 Missing the 21-Day Deadline

If the landlord misses the 21-day deadline entirely, the landlord **forfeits all deduction rights** and must return the full deposit.

### 8.4 Attorney's Fees

Successful tenants may recover reasonable attorney's fees in addition to damages.

### 8.5 Small Claims Court

- California small claims court limit: **$12,500**
- Filing fees: $30-$100
- Tenants commonly pursue security deposit disputes in small claims court
- No attorney representation allowed in small claims (both sides self-represent)

### 8.6 Bad Faith Examples

Courts may find bad faith when landlords:
- Fail to provide itemized statements
- Provide no cost documentation
- Engage in retaliatory withholding
- Overcharge for repairs/cleaning
- Skip required photo documentation and charge for repairs anyway
- Charge for ordinary wear and tear

> **FLAG:** Sources cite the 2x penalty under different subdivision letters -- some say (l), some say (m). The subdivision lettering may have shifted due to the amendments by AB 2801 and SB 611 adding new subdivisions. Product should reference the penalty by description ("bad faith statutory damages up to twice the deposit") rather than by subdivision letter. Verify against current statutory text.

Sources: [ReclaimDeposit](https://www.reclaimdeposit.com/laws/california), [Chugh](https://www.chugh.com/news/new-recording-requirements-for-rental-security-deposits-under-ab-2801), [Lonut Guides](https://lonutguides.com/blog/bad-faith-security-deposit-california), [Coastal Shield Inspections](https://coastalshieldinspections.com/resource-ab-2801-landlord-checklist), [Astanehe Law](https://astanehelaw.com/2025/11/12/all-about-california-civil-code-%C2%A7-1950-5-the-california-security-deposit-law-2025-update/)

## 9. Exemptions and Scope

### 9.1 What Properties Civil Code 1950.5 Applies To

Section 1950.5(a): "This section applies to security for a rental agreement for **residential property that is used as the dwelling of the tenant**."

Covered properties include:
- Apartments
- Single-family homes
- Condominiums
- Duplexes
- Other residential rental units
- Multifamily buildings (rule applies per unit, not per building)

### 9.2 What Properties Are Exempt

Civil Code 1950.5 does **NOT** apply to:
- **Commercial leases** -- governed by lease terms and general contract law
- **Transient occupancy** -- Hotels or motels with occupancy of less than 30 days
- **Certain specialized housing arrangements** with separate governing rules (not further specified in sources)

### 9.3 Owner-Occupied Properties

> **FLAG:** No source found provides a clear, explicit owner-occupied exemption from Civil Code 1950.5. Unlike AB 1482 (Tenant Protection Act), which has explicit exemptions for owner-occupied single-family homes, 1950.5 appears to apply broadly to all residential tenancies. Some sources reference "certain owner-occupied situations with specific exemptions" but do not elaborate. This requires verification against the statutory text. Do NOT assume an owner-occupied exemption exists for AB 2801/1950.5 without attorney confirmation.

### 9.4 Retroactivity / Existing Leases

AB 2801 is **NOT fully retroactive** but some provisions apply to existing tenancies:

| Provision | Retroactive? |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Move-out photo requirements** (April 1, 2025) | YES -- applies to any deductions taken after April 1, 2025, regardless of when tenancy began |
| **Move-in photo requirements** (July 1, 2025) | NO -- applies only to tenancies beginning on or after July 1, 2025 |
| **Repair cost limitations** | YES -- applies to all deductions after January 1, 2025 |
| **Cleaning restrictions** | YES -- applies to all deductions after January 1, 2025 |
| **Pre-move-out inspection enhancement** | YES -- applies to all inspections after January 1, 2025 |

### 9.5 Short-Term Rentals

Per Coastal Shield Inspections: "AB 2801 applies to traditional long-term residential tenancies; nightly rentals typically have platform-specific claims processes." Short-term vacation rentals (e.g., Airbnb, VRBO) are likely outside scope but this is not explicitly addressed in the statute.

### 9.6 Eviction Exception

The "surprise deduction" prohibition in subdivision (f)(4) does NOT apply to evictions under Code of Civil Procedure Sections 1161(2), (3), or (4) (unlawful detainer for nonpayment, nuisance, or illegal activity).

Sources: [xCounsel](https://www.xcounsel.org/security-deposit-demand-letter/california-civil-code-1950-5-explained), [Coastal Shield Inspections](https://coastalshieldinspections.com/resource-ab-2801-landlord-checklist), [CA Legislature](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billTextClient.xhtml?bill_id=202320240AB2801)

## 10. Practical Guidance from Legal Sources

### 10.1 Attorney Recommendations

- **Train all property managers and leasing agents** on photo documentation procedures before tenant possession and after lease termination notice ([Chugh](https://www.chugh.com/news/new-recording-requirements-for-rental-security-deposits-under-ab-2801))
- **Always offer the pre-move-out inspection in writing**, even if the tenant is unlikely to request one. Document the offer. ([Coastal Shield](https://coastalshieldinspections.com/resource-ab-2801-landlord-checklist))
- **Use same camera angles** at move-in and move-out for direct comparison
- **Separate deduction categories**: cleaning, damage, and unpaid rent should be separate line items with separate receipts
- **Calculate depreciation**: charge pro-rated value based on useful life, not full replacement cost ([Coastal Shield](https://coastalshieldinspections.com/resource-ab-2801-landlord-checklist))
- **Document self-performed labor** with hours and hourly rate (e.g., "Three hours at $40/hr, kitchen deep clean" is defensible; "Cleaning -- $120" is not)
- **Consider third-party inspectors** for neutral-witness documentation that is harder for tenants to challenge

### 10.2 Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Sending photos via SMS/WhatsApp** -- strips EXIF metadata
2. **Skipping the pre-move-out inspection** -- California Civil Code 1950.5(f) gives tenant the right to request a walkthrough; offer it every time
3. **Combining deduction categories** into lump sums instead of itemizing
4. **Charging full replacement cost** instead of depreciated value
5. **Undocumented self-labor** -- must record hours and hourly rate
6. **Charging for routine carpet cleaning** without documenting specific damage beyond normal wear
7. **Adding deductions not identified during pre-move-out inspection** (now barred if tenant possessions did not obscure the condition)
8. **Missing the 21-day deadline** -- forfeits all deduction rights
9. **Taking photos days after tenant departure** instead of immediately
10. **No backup storage** for photos -- single-device storage risks data loss

### 10.3 Recommended Room-by-Room Photo Checklist

Coastal Shield Inspections provides a comprehensive room-by-room checklist covering:
- Entry and hallway (door, hardware, walls, flooring, smoke detector)
- Living room (walls, ceiling, windows, blinds, flooring, outlets, HVAC)
- Kitchen (all appliances interior/exterior, countertops, backsplash, sink, cabinets, flooring)
- Bathrooms (toilet, vanity, tub/shower, grout/caulk, tile, exhaust fan, flooring)
- Bedrooms (walls, ceiling, carpet, closet, windows, smoke detector)
- Laundry/utility (hookups, dryer vent, appliance footprint, walls)
- Exterior/yard (door, porch, driveway, landscaping, garage, fencing)
- Mechanical (water heater, HVAC, electrical panel, smoke/CO detectors)

Full checklist: [Coastal Shield Inspections](https://coastalshieldinspections.com/resource-ab-2801-landlord-checklist)

- **CAA Form CA-292:** Waiver of documentation (updated to include photo waiver)
- **SCRHA Form #272:** Move-In/Move-Out Inspection Report
- **SCRHA Form #610:** Itemization of Security Deposit

## 11. Related and Subsequent Legislation

### 11.1 AB 12 (2023) -- Security Deposit Cap

- **Effective July 1, 2024**
- Reduced maximum security deposit to **one month's rent** for all residential units (furnished or unfurnished)
- **Small landlord exception:** Natural persons (or all-natural-person LLCs, or family trusts) owning no more than 2 properties with 4 total units may collect up to 2 months' rent
- Active-duty military: always capped at 1 month, no exceptions
- Pet deposits, last month's rent all count toward the cap
- Nonrefundable deposits remain void

Source: [Rioo App](https://riooapp.com/blog/california-security-deposit-laws)

### 11.2 SB 611 (2024) -- Companion Bill

- **Signed September 19, 2024** (same day as AB 2801)
- Also amends Civil Code 1950.5
- AB 2801 Section 2.5 contains merged provisions that apply if both bills are enacted and AB 2801 is signed last
- Key SB 611 provisions:
  - **Service member protections** (subdivision (c)(4)): higher deposits for service members must be explained in writing; excess must be returned within 6 months if rent is current
  - Additional strengthening of deduction limitations

Source: [CA Legislature](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billTextClient.xhtml?bill_id=202320240AB2801), [Astanehe Law](https://astanehelaw.com/2024/12/12/how-does-californias-security-deposit-law-civil-code-%C2%A7-1950-5-change-in-2024-2025-beyond/)

### 11.3 AB 414 (2025) -- Electronic Security Deposit Refunds

- **Signed October 6, 2025; effective January 1, 2026**
- Amends Civil Code 1950.5 to modernize refund delivery:
  - If landlord received deposit or rent payments **electronically**, refund must be returned **electronically** (unless parties agree otherwise in writing)
  - Landlord must notify tenant in writing of right to electronic refund
  - Itemized statements may be sent by **email** with written tenant consent; otherwise first-class mail or personal delivery
  - Multiple adult tenants: refund issued as one payment payable to all adults unless they consent to split

Source: [Zego](https://www.gozego.com/articles/electronic-security-deposit-refunds-california/), [ReLISTO](https://www.relisto.com/2026/01/12/alert-update-to-california-security-deposit-law-ab-414-now-in-effect/)

### 11.4 Other Recent 1950.5 Amendments

- **AB 1482 (2019):** Tenant Protection Act -- rent caps and just-cause eviction (separate from 1950.5 but relevant context)
- **SB 567 (2023):** Anti-fraud provisions for owner-move-in evictions (not directly amending 1950.5)

## 12. Court Interpretations

**No notable court cases interpreting AB 2801 specifically were found in research as of May 2026.**

This is not surprising given:
- The move-out photo provisions only took effect April 1, 2025 (approximately 13 months ago)
- The move-in photo provisions only took effect July 1, 2025 (approximately 10 months ago)
- Small claims cases (where most deposit disputes are heard) do not typically generate published opinions
- Any superior court cases would still be working through the system

The pre-existing bad faith penalty (2x damages) under 1950.5 has extensive case law, but AB 2801-specific interpretations are still emerging.

## 13. Ambiguities and Conflicts

### 13.1 Confirmed Ambiguities

1. **"Photographs" vs. video:** The statute says "photographs." It does not mention video. It is unclear whether video walkthroughs satisfy the requirement. Best practice: take still photographs AND supplement with video.

2. **"Immediately before, or at the inception of" the tenancy:** The exact timing window is not defined. Does "immediately before" mean the day before? The week before? Best practice: photograph within 7 days of tenant taking possession, ideally same day.

3. **"Within a reasonable time" after move-out:** Not defined. Must be before any repairs begin. Best practice: photograph immediately upon taking possession back, same day if possible.

4. **Photo format/resolution:** The statute is silent. No minimum resolution, no format requirement, no explicit timestamp mandate. Industry sources recommend date-stamped, high-resolution images with EXIF metadata intact, but the statute does not prescribe this.

5. **Owner-occupied exemption:** Not clearly addressed. Civil Code 1950.5 applies to all "residential property used as the dwelling of the tenant." Owner-occupied rentals (e.g., renting a room) appear to be covered, but this needs attorney confirmation.

6. **Subdivision lettering for penalty:** Sources disagree on whether the bad faith penalty is in subdivision (l) or (m). This is likely due to SB 611/AB 2801 adding new subdivisions that shifted the lettering.

7. **Tenant waiver scope:** CAA says tenants can waive photo documentation. It is unclear whether this waiver could be challenged as unconscionable if included as a standard lease term vs. offered separately at move-out.

### 13.2 Source Conflicts

1. **Effective date language:** One source (Chugh) states the law "took full effect on July 1, 2025." This is misleading -- the January 1, 2025 provisions (repair cost limits, cleaning restrictions) took effect earlier. The July 1, 2025 date is when the final provision (move-in photos) kicked in.

2. **Interest rate on deposits:** One source (Israel Samuels) mentions "5.0% annually" for interest on deposits. This is NOT a statewide requirement -- deposit interest is required only in specific jurisdictions (San Francisco, Los Angeles, Berkeley, West Hollywood, etc.) and rates vary by locality.

3. **Retention period for photos:** One source recommends 4 years. No statutory retention period was found. The statute of limitations for breach of contract (4 years) and the small claims filing window are relevant considerations.

4. **$125 threshold:** Multiple sources confirm the $125 documentation threshold. One source (Israel Samuels) mentions a waiver provision; verify this is in the current statutory text and not a pre-amendment version.
